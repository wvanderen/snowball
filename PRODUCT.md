# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Snowball is for people who want to keep important life domains in motion without maintaining a heavy productivity system. It is especially designed for users who struggle with consistency, context switching, schedule adherence, or ADHD-style re-entry friction. They may arrive tired, distracted, or returning after missed days; the product should make partial effort feel legitimate and make starting again emotionally safe.

## Product Purpose

Snowball is a local-first, mobile-first activity tracker disguised as an incremental lattice game. Its core promise is simple: tap a life sphere, focus for a while, and watch real consistency become momentum, energy, XP, and a growing personal sigil. Success means the user can start a real-world activity quickly, see daily progress clearly, and feel pulled back by playful progression rather than pushed by shame or administrative burden.

## Brand Personality

Strategic, motivating, playful. Snowball should feel like tending a living network: calm enough for daily use, game-like enough to make progress feel charged, and thoughtful enough that choices in the lattice feel meaningful. The tone should encourage without scolding, celebrate small returns, and frame consistency as something that can bend, recover, and grow.

## Anti-references

Snowball should not feel like a generic productivity app, task manager, calendar, habit checklist, or streak-pressure dashboard. Avoid sterile SaaS panels, corporate wellness aesthetics, punitive red failure states, endless configuration, and interfaces that imply the user is behind. The game layer should not become so noisy or optimization-heavy that real life feels secondary to playing correctly.

## Design Principles

1. Start first: the primary path is open app, tap sphere, begin. Planning, editing, and optimization must stay secondary.
2. Make partial effort count: short sessions, missed-day recovery, and imperfect returns should be recognized as real progress.
3. Let the lattice explain itself: the home screen should become a readable personal sigil where momentum, milestone progress, active sessions, and energy flow are visible at a glance.
4. Reward consistency without worshiping streaks: streaks may support some builds, but they are not the main measure of worth or success.
5. Unfold depth gradually: strategic systems such as rituals, routes, glyphs, upgrades, and recovery rituals should add long-term play without adding daily-use friction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific formal accessibility bar has been set yet. Snowball should still treat accessibility as part of the core product: preserve strong contrast, avoid color-only status communication, support reduced-motion preferences, keep tap targets comfortable on mobile, and use non-shaming copy for missed days, decay, recovery, and incomplete milestones.
