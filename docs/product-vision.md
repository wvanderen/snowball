# Snowball Product Vision

## One-line pitch

Snowball is a stripped-down activity tracker disguised as an incremental lattice game: tap a life sphere, focus for a while, and watch real consistency turn into momentum, energy, and a growing personal sigil.

## Core promise

Snowball helps users keep important life domains in motion by making the act of starting nearly frictionless and making consistency visually, emotionally, and mechanically rewarding.

The app should feel less like maintaining a productivity system and more like tending a living network that grows because the user keeps showing up.

## Target user

Snowball is especially designed for people who struggle with consistency, context switching, and schedule adherence, including ADHD users. The app should support partial effort, easy re-entry, and non-punitive recovery after missed days.

## Product pillars

### 1. Start first

The primary interaction is:

```txt
open app -> tap sphere -> session starts
```

Configuration, planning, editing, and optimization should stay secondary to beginning an activity.

### 2. Consistency over intensity

Snowball rewards recurring engagement more than heroic bursts. Partial sessions matter, daily milestones matter, and momentum should bend rather than shatter.

### 3. Real life first, game second

The tracking layer must remain simple and emotionally safe. The game layer can become deep, strategic, and expressive, but it should never block the user from quickly starting a real activity.

### 4. A personal sigil

The home screen is a network of spheres around a center node. Over time, it should bloom from two nodes into a meaningful visual representation of the user's life domains, habits, and strategic choices.

### 5. Playful strategy without shame

Snowball should borrow the engagement loops of incremental games without making the user feel punished for being human. Streaks can exist, but they are one build path, not the central source of worth.

## Concept model

### Center Sphere

The Center Sphere is the core/output node of the lattice.

Early role:

- home anchor
- energy destination/storage
- upgrade hub
- visual center of the sigil

Later role:

- rest/recovery rituals
- centering activities
- global upgrades
- lattice-wide modifiers

### Domain Spheres

A sphere represents a flexible life domain/container, not a rigid habit.

Examples:

- Music
- Fitness
- Writing
- Home
- Study
- Social
- Rest

A sphere has a daily milestone, momentum, accumulated time, rituals, production, and connections.

### Rituals

A ritual is a concrete activity inside a sphere.

Examples inside Music:

- Guitar practice
- Ableton session
- Ear training
- Songwriting

Only one ritual is active at a time. A sphere can eventually have a small list of favorite rituals for quick switching. Any ritual inside a sphere contributes to that sphere's daily milestone.

### Sessions

A session is a tracked period of activity. Sessions may be shorter than the daily target and still count toward XP, energy, progress, and momentum.

### Daily milestones

A daily milestone is a sphere-level requirement, initially time-based.

Example:

```txt
Music daily milestone: 20 minutes
Morning guitar: 8 minutes
Evening Ableton: 12 minutes
Milestone complete
```

Partial progress counts toward the daily milestone, but the milestone boost only triggers once the full target is reached.

## Key mechanics

### Momentum

Momentum is the primary consistency health metric. It ranges from 0 to 100, rises through activity, rises more on milestone completion, and decays gradually when neglected.

Momentum should replace punitive streak pressure as the default emotional model.

### Streaks

Streaks are tracked but not central. Later glyphs/builds may reward streaks, but strong non-streak builds should also exist.

### XP

Experience represents real focused effort.

Initial rule:

```txt
1 focused minute = 1 XP
```

XP should remain relatively grounded and should not scale wildly.

### Energy

Energy is the main game currency and visual flow.

It can be used for:

- sphere slots
- sphere upgrades
- connection upgrades
- glyph slots
- center upgrades
- later progression systems

Energy can scale through game mechanics.

### Passive production

Spheres generate passive energy while idle and while the app is closed. Passive production depends on level, momentum, and later glyphs/connections.

### Active production

Active production happens while a session is running. It should be much stronger than passive production and eventually affect the broader network.

Active state can later:

- increase direct output
- buff connected spheres
- charge glyphs
- alter routing
- trigger temporary lattice effects

### Milestone boost

When a sphere's daily milestone is reached, the sphere gets a discrete reward/bloom.

Possible rewards:

- energy burst
- momentum boost
- visual bloom
- streak/stat update
- glyph triggers

## Visual direction

Snowball should be mobile-first, dark, clean, mystical, and game-like without becoming visually noisy.

Important visual signals:

- sphere glow = momentum
- progress ring = today's milestone progress
- pulse = active session
- flowing line = energy routing
- center node = output/core
- bloom = milestone completion
- expanding lattice = long-term growth

## UX principles

- Tap to start immediately.
- Partial effort is always meaningful.
- Missed days should invite recovery, not shame.
- Configuration should be discoverable but not foregrounded.
- Game depth should unfold gradually.
- The user should benefit even if they ignore optimization.
